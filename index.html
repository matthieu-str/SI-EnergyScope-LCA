Regionalized and prospective LCA in energy system optimization

This dashboard presents results from the paper "Regionalized and prospective life-cycle assessment in energy system optimization: effects on environmental profiling and energy planning for net-zero without burden shifting", submitted to Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews.

Paper
Souttre M., Chuat A., Schnidrig J., Fischer L., Maj J., Majeau-Bettez G., Maréchal F., Margni M. (2026). Regionalized and prospective life-cycle assessment in energy system optimization: effects on environmental profiling and energy planning for net-zero without burden shifting. Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews.

The study applies a novel framework to endogenously integrate prospective and regionalized LCA impact scores into an energy system model (ESM) — specifically EnergyScope — adapted to the province of Québec. The central research question is: how does the integration of prospective scenarios and regionalization in LCA affect energy system environmental profiling and energy planning decision-making?

The case study targets a territorial net-zero emissions energy system for Québec by 2050, without burden-shifting to other environmental impact categories or outsourcing emissions abroad. Results are obtained for two contrasting background IAM scenarios (SSP5-H and SSP2-L) combined with five levels of LCA regionalization.

Prospective-regionalization modeling levels

Results are produced for the following combinations of prospective and regionalization assumptions. The Def. level is the 2050 reference without IAM projections or spatial differentiation; all other levels include IAM background projections from the IMAGE model.

Label IAM background Spatialization Foreground reg. Background reg. Description
Def. — — — — Default ecoinvent database, no prospective adjustments, no spatial differentiation.
IAM ✓ — — — Background inventory updated with IMAGE SSP projections via premise. No spatial differentiation.
IAM + Spat. ✓ ✓ — — Elementary flows spatialized to their emission location; regionalized characterization factors applied (IMPACT World+).
IAM + Spat. + Fore. ✓ ✓ ✓ — First-tier flows of operation and resource LCI datasets relocated to Québec using the foreground regionalization module of mescal.
IAM + Spat. + Back. ✓ ✓ — ✓ National production and consumption markets added via regioinvent (~134 000 additional datasets based on BACI trade data).
IAM + Spat. + Fore. + Back. ✓ ✓ ✓ ✓ Most comprehensive level: full spatialization, foreground and background regionalization, and IAM prospective assumptions.

Each level is run under two SSP-RCP scenarios: SSP5-H (~+3.4°C by 2100, conservative background, chosen following the precautionary principle) and SSP2-L (~+1.6°C by 2100, strong global mitigation). IAM: IMAGE model. Spat.: spatialization of elementary flows and impact regionalization. Fore.: foreground inventory regionalization. Back.: background inventory regionalization.

Environmental impact categories

LCA scores are calculated using the regionalized version of IMPACT World+ (v2.1). Results are reported for the following categories across the comparative and per-level views:

Category Type Unit Description
Total human health Endpoint DALY/(cap.yr) Sum of all endpoint categories contributing to human health damage, including climate change.
Total ecosystem quality Endpoint PDF.m².yr/(cap.yr) Sum of all endpoint categories contributing to ecosystem quality damage, including climate change.
Climate change (total) Midpoint t CO₂-eq/(cap.yr) Short-term climate change impact (GWP100). Uses −1/+1 biogenic carbon accounting for CDR technologies.
Remaining human health Remaining DALY/(cap.yr) Human health damage excluding short-term and long-term climate change categories. Used as constraint to prevent burden shifting.
Remaining ecosystem quality Remaining PDF.m².yr/(cap.yr) Ecosystem quality damage excluding short-term and long-term climate change categories. Used as constraint to prevent burden shifting.
Climate change — territorial Territorial t CO₂-eq/(cap.yr) Life-cycle GHG impacts from emissions occurring within Québec. Constrained to zero (net-zero target).
Climate change — abroad Abroad t CO₂-eq/(cap.yr) Life-cycle GHG impacts from emissions occurring outside Québec (supply chain). Constrained to ≤ 2023 equivalent.

Tools and methods

The workflow combines three open-source Python tools with the EnergyScope optimization model:

mescal premise regioinvent regiopremise EnergyScope-Québec ecoinvent 3.10.1 (cut-off) IMPACT World+ v2.1 IMAGE (IAM) · SSP5-H / SSP2-L

mescal handles systematic harmonization between EnergyScope and LCI datasets: double-counting removal, parameter alignment, foreground regionalization, and LCIA score normalization. premise generates prospective ecoinvent databases from IAM outputs. regioinvent adds ~134 000 regionalized market datasets based on BACI international trade data. regiopremise ensures compatibility between these two tools.

Using this application

This application has three main views:

Comparative view — Browse energy system configurations and environmental impact profiles across all prospective-regionalization levels simultaneously. Use the sidebar to select a chart category. For electricity, a unit toggle (TWh/yr ↔ GW) is available.

Per-level view — Inspect the energy and carbon flow Sankey diagrams for a specific prospective-regionalization level and SSP scenario. Use the dropdown to select a configuration and the tabs to toggle between flow types.

Sensitivity analysis — Explore how the energy system configuration changes as a function of industrial carbon capture and storage availability (7–32 Mt CO₂/yr). Select the modeling level and SSP scenario; the slider controls the carbon capture value for which the Sankey diagrams are shown.

All chart files are expected in a Results/ subdirectory relative to this HTML file, and sensitivity Sankeys in Results/sensitivity/. File naming conventions follow the pattern shown in each placeholder frame. Charts are generated with Plotly from the EnergyScope-LCA Python post-processing pipeline.
